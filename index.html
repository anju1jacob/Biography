Former President of India : K. R. Narayanan

K. R. Narayanan, full name Kocheril Raman Narayanan, was born on October 27, 1920, in the village of Perumthanam, Uzhavoor, in the state of Kerala, India. He rose from humble beginnings to become the first Dalit President of India, a testament to his determination, intellect, and commitment to public service. Here is an overview of his life and career:

Early Life and Education

Narayanan was born into a Dalit family, traditionally considered a lower caste in the Indian social hierarchy. Despite the socioeconomic challenges, his parents ensured that he received a good education. He completed his early education in his hometown before moving to Kottayam for higher studies. He pursued a Bachelor of Arts degree in English Literature from University College, Thiruvananthapuram, under the University of Kerala, and then obtained a Master of Arts degree in English Literature from the University of Madras. Narayanan was a brilliant student, securing high ranks in his academic pursuits.

Professional Beginnings

After his initial education in India, Narayanan won a scholarship to study in the United Kingdom. He attended the London School of Economics (LSE) and studied under the renowned political scientist Harold Laski. He earned a Bachelor of Science degree in Economics with specialization in political science. Upon his return to India, Narayanan initially worked as a journalist for The Hindu and later for the Times of India. He also briefly taught at the University of Calcutta.

Diplomatic Career

Narayanan joined the Indian Foreign Service (IFS) in 1949. His diplomatic career was distinguished, serving in various capacities in embassies and high commissions around the world, including in Rangoon, Tokyo, London, Canberra, and Hanoi. He also served as India's ambassador to Thailand, Turkey, and China.

Political Career

Encouraged by Prime Minister Indira Gandhi, Narayanan entered politics and was elected to the Indian Parliament from the Ottapalam constituency in Kerala in 1984 as a member of the Indian National Congress (INC). He served as a Member of Parliament for three consecutive terms. During his tenure in Parliament, Narayanan held several important positions, including Minister of State for Planning, Minister of State for External Affairs, and Minister of State for Science and Technology.

Vice Presidency and Presidency

In 1992, Narayanan was elected as the ninth Vice President of India. His tenure was marked by his commitment to upholding constitutional values and his deep understanding of the political landscape of India. In 1997, Narayanan was elected as the tenth President of India, becoming the first Dalit to hold the highest constitutional office in the country. His presidency was notable for his advocacy for social justice, equality, and the empowerment of marginalized communities. He was known for his principled stand on various issues and his emphasis on the importance of a secular, democratic, and inclusive India.

Legacy

Narayanan's life and career are seen as a beacon of hope and inspiration for millions of marginalized people in India. He is remembered for his integrity, intellect, and his tireless efforts to promote social justice and equality. His contributions to Indian diplomacy and politics have left an indelible mark on the country. K. R. Narayanan passed away on November 9, 2005. His life story continues to inspire and motivate future generations to strive for excellence despite adversity.
